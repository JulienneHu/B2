    Listening and Speaking

    Text Analysis

    The teaching content of this lesson is selected from the "14th Five-Year Plan" vocational education national planning textbook published by Higher Education Press, the listening and speaking part of Unit 1 "Travel" of "English 2 Basic Module". Students are required to understand hotel reservation dialogues, record key information for hotel room reservations, introduce tourist attractions, and recommend suitable travel routes based on customer needs.

    Student Analysis

    The theme of this unit is travel, which is closely related to students' lives. Students are generally very interested in different customs and practices, and the vast majority of students have travel experiences both domestically and abroad. Students can briefly introduce attractions in English, but their ability to describe attractions in detail needs improvement, and they cannot accurately introduce the characteristics of travel destinations to others. In terms of preparing for travel topics, such as booking rooms, students lack experience. Therefore, teachers design teaching activities to guide students in learning travel-related vocabulary and sentences, learning dialogues to introduce attraction information to others, practicing substitutions, and then using the learned travel-related language knowledge to introduce attractions to "tourists".

    Instructional Objectives

    1.Students can understand dialogues about hotel room reservations and obtain key information.
    2.Students can combine model sentences and given key information to complete descriptions of tourist destinations.
    3.Students can recommend reasonable travel products based on customer travel needs.
    4.Students can understand and describe the general situation of some famous domestic and international tourist destinations.

    Key and Difficult Points

    Teaching Focus: Accurately describing the general situation of travel destinations.
    Teaching Difficulties: Recommending reasonable travel products based on customer needs.

    Teaching Procedure

    Step 1: Warming Up
    1. Think and Match
    Handling Teaching Activity 1 can be divided into two steps: *
    (1) Brainstorm: Activate vocabulary reserves. Students speak out about travel destinations and famous attractions they have visited. The teacher can provide appropriate pictures as prompts to activate student thinking.
    (2) Provide four pictures and country names, ask students to observe carefully, and match the text with the pictures based on existing knowledge. Ask students "Which country is this?" and provide feedback on their responses.
    2. Tick and Discuss
    Handling Teaching Activity 2 can be divided into three steps:
    (1) Students view pictures and familiarize themselves with related vocabulary, laying the groundwork for language knowledge.
    (2) Ask peers what preparations they make before traveling.
    The teacher can provide key sentences:
    What will you prepare before traveling?
    I often---/ I will---
    What about you?
    Sample answer:
    A: The summer holiday is coming. What do you have to prepare before traveling?
    B: I often search for local scenic spots with my smartphone. What about you?
    A: I will book tickets and hotel rooms online.
    (3) Group activities: Discuss and collect what preparations people need to make before traveling, and then make group presentations. (Design intent: This section aims to stimulate students' interest in this unit through brainstorming, peer dialogue, group discussions, etc., while mastering relevant vocabulary and expressions, laying the language and background knowledge foundation for listening activities.)
    Step 2: Listening
    1.Listen and Tick
    Handling Teaching Activity 3. Students read the options before listening to familiarize themselves with them. Then they listen to the recording and make choices. The teacher checks and provides feedback after listening.
    2. Listen and Decide Handling
    Teaching Activity 4. Students first read the options and make preliminary judgments. Then they listen to the recording, grasp keywords, and select the correct option. The teacher checks and provides feedback after listening.
    3. Listen and Complete Handling
    Teaching Activity 5 can be divided into three steps:
    (1) Students browse the information to be filled in the reservation card (guest name, gender, room type, and personal preferences).
    (2) Listen to the recording and fill in the blanks based on the given prompt words: First Name, Gender, Types of room, The guest’s preferences.
    (3) After the listening ends, the teacher checks and verifies the answers, presents the listening text, and briefly analyzes the key information. (Design intent: This section aims to train students' ability to grasp key information while helping them understand the information needed to book a room before traveling and the key sentence patterns that can be used.)
    Step 3: Speaking
    1. Listen, Read, and Underline
    Handling Teaching Activity 6 can be divided into three steps:
    (1) The teacher introduces the dialogue background, students listen to the recording, imitate the pronunciation and intonation, and underline the sentences related to discussing travel destinations. The teacher provides answers. Then, two students are invited to demonstrate the dialogue and receive feedback.

    Step 4: Conclusion

    Reading and Writing

    Text Analysis

    The teaching content of this lesson is selected from the "Reading and Writing" section of Unit 1 of the textbook. It requires students to read articles, become familiar with and master the stylistic and linguistic characteristics of biographical articles introducing individuals' life stories; through observation, analysis, and comparison, learn the narrative methods of stories about individuals, clarify the context of articles, and exercise the ability to extract and summarize textual information. By using a timeline to become familiar with the life of Xu Xiake and combining it with his life experiences and outstanding contributions, students can perceive the great charm of historical figures, inspire themselves to set lofty aspirations, and dare to make efforts for them.

    Student Analysis

    After two previous lessons, students have already mastered vocabulary and sentences related to travel. They can converse with peers and introduce information about scenic spots to them. By researching materials for homework, students have learned about the life stories and major achievements of some world-famous travel adventurers. However, most of this information exists in fragmented form, incomplete, and disjointed. Most students have not read biographical articles in English about individuals' life stories and are not familiar with the characteristics and linguistic features of this type of genre.

    Instructional Objectives

    1.Students can understand biographical articles about individuals' life stories and, through observation, analysis, and comparison, understand the writing characteristics of biographical articles introducing individuals' life stories.
    2.Learn to use the skimming reading strategy and use a timeline to extract key information from texts introducing Xu Xiake's life experiences.
    3.Students can extract information from texts introducing individuals and complete promotional posters.
    4.Understand the roles of Xu Xiake and Marco Polo in promoting human civilization. By understanding the travel experiences, indomitable will, and contributions to future generations of two travel adventurers from different countries, enhance awareness of the importance of cross-cultural exchanges between different countries.

    Key and Difficult Points

    Teaching Focus: Becoming familiar with the writing characteristics of biographical articles about individuals' life stories, using the skimming reading strategy to extract information from texts introducing individuals, and completing promotional posters.
    Teaching Difficulties: Combining the requirements of the poster, extracting main information from texts introducing Xu Xiake and Marco Polo to complete the poster.

    Teaching Procedure

    Step 1: Warming Up
    *1. Presentation:
    (1) Select some students to present the dialogues from the first part of the homework, and the teacher provides feedback.
    (2) The teacher selects representatives from some groups to present the main achievements of "World-Famous Travel Adventurers."
    (3) Teacher feedback and group voting to select the favorite explorer, followed by a brief explanation of reasons.
    (Design Intent: Review the content from the previous two lessons to assess student learning outcomes. Activate students' knowledge about travel adventurers while laying the groundwork for the upcoming reading and writing activities.)
    Step 2: Pre-reading
    1. Think and discuss:
    Deal with textbook Activity 9, which can be divided into two steps:
    (1) Group discussion: Based on the presentations just given by classmates and their own experiences, discuss why people travel?
    (2) The teacher asks some students to speak, summarizing and refining students' viewpoints.
    (Design Intent: Encourage students to think about the purposes of traveling, introduce the article's topic, stimulate student interest, and cultivate their thinking abilities.)
    Step 3: While-reading
    1. Read and choose:
    Deal with textbook Activity 10, which can be divided into three steps:
    (1) Students quickly read the options to understand the meaning and focus of each option.
    (2) Students quickly read the article, grasp key sentences, and select the article title.
    (3) The teacher checks attendance and asks students to explain their reasons and provides feedback.
    2. Read and complete:
    Deal with textbook Activity 11, which can be divided into three steps:
    (1) The teacher guides students in learning the reading strategy: skimming. To obtain the main information of the article, skimming (quick reading) can be used. According to the prompts of the timeline, quickly find relevant events from the article.
    (2) Students skim the article and fill in the blanks to complete the timeline of events.
    (3) The teacher checks attendance and provides feedback.
    Reference Answers:
    1606: Xu’s father died and he had to take care of his mother
    1609: Xu set off for the first time with the support of his family
    3. Choose and talk:
    Deal with textbook Activity 12, which can be divided into two steps:
    (1) Students read the article carefully, identify the key factors contributing to Xu Xiake's success, and compare them with the options in Activity 12 to select the correct answer.
    (2) The teacher asks questions, requiring students to state the options, explain their reasons, and provide feedback.
    Answers:
    - Having the family support
    - Being interested in reading books
    - Holding on to his dreams
    - Being a careful man
    - Having a passion for traveling
    4. Read and match:
    Deal with textbook Activity 13, which can be divided into three steps:
    (1) Read Activity 13 questions and options, select the correct answers.
    (2) Practice question and answer exercises with peers based on the questions and answers in Activity 13.
    *You can also follow the introduction methods of Xu Xiake and Marco Polo. Introduce your favorite traveler's life, major achievements, and works based on the information searched before class.
    (3) The teacher asks some students to imitate the introduction methods in the textbook to introduce Marco Polo or other travelers.
    (Design Intent: This activity aims to help students practice reading strategies, such as scanning to extract central ideas, summarizing the main theme of the article; skimming: using a timeline to extract key information, organizing Xu Xiake's life; students think and discuss Xu Xiake's key success factors from the text to exercise thinking skills; briefly compare Xu Xiake with Marco Polo or other travelers to expand cultural perspectives.)
    Step 4: Writing
    1. Read and complete:
    Deal with textbook Activity 13, which can be divided into four steps:
    (1) Students reread the article and Activity 13 content, reviewing the main information about Xu Xiake and Marco Polo.
    *(2) Guide students in group discussions and answer the following questions:
    - Who was Xu Xiake/Marco Polo?
    - At what age did he set out on his first trip?
    - What are his chief achievements?
    The teacher evaluates students' responses and conducts board writing, thereby outlining the writing framework.
    - Xu Xiake was a geographer in the Ming Dynasty.
    - At the age of 22, Xu set off for the first time.
    - He spent over 30 years traveling throughout the country, carefully studying the places he passed through.
    (3) Based on the answers to the questions and the writing framework from the previous step, complete the promotional poster.
    (4) Select representatives from some students to present the posters, and the teacher provides comments and feedback.
    Writing Evaluation Criteria:
    (1) Are spelling and punctuation correct?
    (2) Is grammar used correctly?
    (3) Are relevant vocabulary and sentence patterns used correctly?
    (4) Is the expression clear and logical?
    (Design Intent: This segment is a writing activity where the teacher guides students in repeatedly studying the reading materials, discussing to help students organize key information, and preparing thoroughly for writing; based on a full understanding of the text, complete the Xu Xiake and Marco Polo-themed travelogue exhibition poster filling, focusing on cultivating summarization skills.)
    Step 5: Conclusion
    The teacher guides students to summarize what they have learned in this unit, combining the micro-lessons from the reading and writing sections of the unit.
    Step 6: Homework
    Students continue to collect information about other travelers, following the example of the textbook model, to design promotional posters for these travelers.

    Language Practice & Vocabulary

    Text Analysis

    The main content of this lesson comes from the Language Practice section of Unit 1 in the textbook, which is divided into two parts: Grammar and Vocabulary. The focus is on studying the usage of the past simple tense, the changes in irregular verbs in the past tense, and the usage of key vocabulary in this unit's reading materials. Students will summarize the basic usage of the past simple tense by observing example sentences, identifying, completing sentences, and rewriting short passages. They will practice using the past simple tense to improve their language proficiency. By applying phrases and words in specific contexts, students will reinforce the language points of this unit and achieve the goal of real application.

    Student Analysis

    After studying English in middle school, students have a certain understanding of the past simple tense, but it may not be thorough or systematic. Most students can understand the usage of the past simple tense but may struggle to accurately use it to describe events. While students are familiar with the changes in regular verb past tense, their memorization of irregular verb past tense may not be accurate. Therefore, teachers need to guide students in summarizing, practicing, and memorizing irregular verb past tense to deepen their memory and ultimately achieve the goal of accurately using the past simple tense to describe events.

    Instructional Objectives

    1.Students can summarize the basic usage of the past simple tense by observing example sentences.
    2.With the guidance of the teacher, students can recall the past tense of commonly used irregular verbs
    3.Through exercises like completing sentences, students can accurately apply the past simple tense in specific contexts.
    4.Review key vocabulary and phrases such as "all sorts of," "due to," "set off," "give up," "pass through," etc. Practice using these phrases and words correctly to consolidate the language points of this unit.
    5.By learning and applying grammar knowledge, students can develop good learning habits and improve their language proficiency.

    Key and Difficult Points

    Teaching Focus:
    1.Students can summarize the basic usage of the past simple tense by observing example sentences and recall the past tense of commonly used irregular verbs with the guidance of the teacher. They can accurately use the past simple tense in specific contexts.
    2.Accurately use the key vocabulary of this unit to complete sentence completion exercises.
    Teaching Difficulties:
    Accurately using the past simple tense in specific contexts.

    Teaching Procedure

    Step 1: Warming-up
    *1. Revision:
    Require students to read the text and answer the following questions. Guide students to use the past simple tense correctly:
    Who was Xu Xiake?
    What happened to Xu Xiake when he was 19?
    When did Xu Xiake set off for his journey for the first time?
    How long did he spend traveling?
    What book did Xu Xiake write?
    (Design Intent: Review the previous lesson through reading and answering questions, guiding students to use the past simple tense correctly. Activate students' existing knowledge to prepare for systematic learning and focused explanation of the past simple tense.)
    Step 2: Presentation
    Read and underline:
    Handle textbook Activity 14 in four steps:
    (1) Group activity: Read the sentences in the grammar box, observe the italicized parts, discuss common points, and summarize the basic forms of verb past tense, understanding the usage of the past simple tense.
    Definition: The past simple tense indicates past actions or states that once existed. Verb past tense has two forms: regular and irregular.
    (2) Students read the sentences in Activity 15, underline the past tense of verbs.
    (3) Teacher checks and provides feedback.
    *(4) Brainstorming: Guide students to state the rules of regular verb past tense changes and common irregular verb past tense and write them on the board.

    Culture Understanding &Group Work

    Text Analysis

    The main content of this lesson is Culture Understanding and Group Work in Unit 1. The Culture Understanding activity is an explanatory text that introduces the difference between tourists and travelers. The article has a clear structure and contains a lot of comparative introductions. It comprehensively introduces what constitutes tourists and travelers, covering their identity differences, travel purposes, and different attitudes towards travel. The Group Work requires students to integrate language knowledge learned in this unit to create travel plans based on the travel preferences of tourists and the information provided in the textbook, exercising their ability to perceive differences in thinking.

    Student Analysis

    Through the learning in the previous five lessons, students have gained a deep understanding of topics such as travel, introduction to attractions, and the life stories of travel explorers. They have mastered vocabulary and sentence structures related to travel. They can understand dialogues about booking hotel rooms, introduce corresponding attractions based on prompts, read biographies of characters to understand their writing style and language characteristics. However, they still cannot synthesize the language knowledge learned in this unit to solve specific problems related to travel in professional scenarios.

    Instructional Objectives

    1.To understand the difference between travelers and tourists through reading, perceive the different needs of different people when traveling, and then analyze their own travel preferences and attitudes towards travel.
    2.To obtain the travel wishes of three foreign students through reading.
    3.To use existing life knowledge to classify Beijing attractions based on text and illustrations.
    4.To select suitable attractions for the three students based on their travel preferences and create brief travel plans.
    5.To self-evaluate, summarize, and reflect on learning methods and strategies for this unit, cultivate good learning habits, and improve language learning and application abilities.

    Key and Difficult Points

    Teaching Focus: To create brief travel plans for the three students based on their travel preferences.
    Teaching Difficulties: To synthesize the vocabulary, phrases, and sentence structures related to travel learned in this unit while creating travel plans.

Perceive the different needs of individuals during travel, summarize the differences between tourists and travelers. Analyze personal travel preferences and attitudes towards travel based on one's own situation.)
