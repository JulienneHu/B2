    Listening and Speaking

    Text Analysis

    The content of this lesson is selected from the “Fourteenth Five-Year Plan” vocational education national planning textbook published by the Higher Education Press, “Basic English Module 2,” focusing on Unit 3's listening and speaking section. It requires students to be familiar with the names of internship positions and locations, understand related terminology, and apply learned sentence patterns to discuss their internship experiences.

    Student Analysis

    The target students for this lesson are first-year students at vocational schools. After a semester of study, these students have acquired some basic English knowledge, gradually mastered vocational English learning methods, developed some reading skills, and formed good learning habits, thus gradually increasing their interest in learning. However, there are still some students with less interest, who seldom practice speaking, and whose learning capabilities need improvement. Therefore, teachers can use rich visual content to present activities and exercises in this lesson to activate students’ existing knowledge for teaching.

    Instructional Objectives

    1. Students should be able to understand conversations about internship locations and positions and extract key information.
    2. Students should be able to master vocabulary related to internship positions and engage in simple dialogues about them, such as:
    - agreement, assistant, challenge, guest, colleagues, training, definition, description, etc.
    3. Students should be able to discuss and communicate about internship situations in English based on different contexts:
    How is it going with…?
    I’ve got a problem. Could you please…?

    Key and Difficult Points

    Teaching Focus: To be able to master sentences discussing internship situations.
    Teaching Challenge: To be able to discuss and communicate about internship situations according to different work contexts.

    Teaching Procedure

    Reading and Writing

    Text Analysis

    The content for this lesson is taken from Unit 1's reading and writing section of the textbook, which requires students to understand the specific content related to work projects. This allows students to have a detailed understanding of the projects, and enables them to compare and analyze key information, refine these details, organize internship projects, and practice their reading comprehension and key information extraction skills.

    Student Analysis

    After the previous two lessons, students are now able to discuss different work scenarios, use common sentence patterns to address and resolve issues encountered during internships and training, and possess some ability to summarize and report on their internships.

    Instructional Objectives

    1. Students should be able to understand the specific arrangements of internships and training, and have a detailed understanding of the projects.
    2. Students should be able to accurately use important vocabulary and phrases during their internships and training, such as teamwork, creating resumes, and develop active skills to complete internship briefings.
    3. Students should be able to organize the text structure and clarify the article's context through subtitles and mind maps, training their ability to refine and summarize text information.
    4. Students should be able to develop good learning habits and improve their language learning and application skills through self-evaluation and reflective summaries after class.

    Key and Difficult Points

    Teaching Focus: To be familiar with and master the stylistic features and language characteristics of project description through reading articles.
    Teaching Challenge: To organize important information from the reading materials and extract factual information to support the writing of internship reports.

    Teaching Procedure

    Language Practice & Vocabulary

    Text Analysis

    The main content of this lesson includes the "Language Practice" and "Vocabulary" sections, focusing on the past continuous tense and the key vocabulary of this unit. The past continuous tense is a grammatical topic students frequently encounter. Understanding this tense accurately and being able to apply it proficiently can facilitate learning of future tenses. The key vocabulary is derived from the listening and reading materials of this unit, and teachers can further explain and organize exercises and tests to reinforce the learned content.

    Student Analysis

    Students, having studied English during their middle school years, have some understanding of English grammatical phenomena, but not in depth. Some students find memorizing vocabulary and grammar challenging, leading to a fear of these topics. Helping students overcome this fear and boosting their confidence in learning English is an important task for teachers. Additionally, teachers should encourage active learning in the English classroom, create engaging scenarios, ask questions frequently, and promote extensive practice in vocabulary and grammar.

    Instructional Objectives

    1. Master the basic usage of the past continuous tense and be able to apply it accurately.
    2. Accurately use key vocabulary in specific contexts, such as: was waiting, was brushing, were reading, were running, was working, etc.
    3. Through the study and practical application of grammatical knowledge, students should develop good study habits and improve their language application skills.
    4. By studying the past continuous tense in English, students should develop good study habits and enhance their language application skills.

    Key and Difficult Points

    Teaching Focus: To master the basic form and usage of the past continuous tense and apply it practically.
    Teaching Challenge: To accurately use the past continuous tense in specific contexts.

    Culture Understanding &Group Work

    Text Analysis

    This lesson integrates the "Culture Understanding" and "Group Work" sections of the textbook. The "Culture Understanding" section introduces the Australian Vocational and Technical Colleges, expanding knowledge on foreign vocational education and appreciating its importance in the educational systems abroad. The "Group Work" section guides students to complete real tasks during their internships and training, testing their learning outcomes, further consolidating the language knowledge about internships introduced in this unit, and fostering teamwork and innovation skills. By completing an internship briefing design, students learn to respect the cultural differences between Western and Eastern corporate cultures in practice, understand cultural diversity, and strengthen cultural confidence.

    Student Analysis

    After studying a unit, students have mastered important sentences for communication during internships and training, can conduct simple exchanges about their internships, and report on their experiences. Students are able to use vocabulary and sentence patterns to discuss and resolve issues encountered during internships to summarize a training briefing. However, as students receive less training in internship-related writing on regular days, they face certain difficulties.

    Instructional Objectives

    1. Accurately understand the content of articles, be able to extract each intern's characteristics, match interns with their performance records, select outstanding interns, and complete and present group briefings.
    2. Learn to express evaluations in simple English through completing briefing designs.
    3. Through self-evaluation, summarize learning methods and strategies, develop good learning habits, and improve language learning and application skills.

    Key and Difficult Points

    Teaching Focus: Learn to describe relevant sentences about internships and training, and complete webpage design.
    Teaching Challenge: Complete the internship briefing design through information collection, discussion, and collaboration.
